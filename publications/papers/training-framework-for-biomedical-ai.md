We present a retrospective analysis of the Magami OS training framework, which has reached over 200 biomedical researchers across 12 African countries. The curriculum covers foundational machine learning, molecular modelling, high-performance computing, and ethical AI practices. We report quantitative outcomes including pre- and post-training assessment scores, successfully completed collaborative projects, and participant-reported impact on their research workflows. Key lessons include the importance of context-aware curriculum design, the effectiveness of mentorship models, and the critical role of sustained infrastructure support.
