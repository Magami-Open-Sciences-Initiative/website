Artificial intelligence is reshaping how we discover new drugs. For researchers in Africa, this presents a unique opportunity to leapfrog traditional bottlenecks and tackle diseases that have long been neglected by the global pharmaceutical industry.

## What is AI-driven drug discovery?

At its core, AI helps researchers:

- **Analyse vast datasets** — genomic, proteomic, and clinical data
- **Predict molecular interactions** — before costly lab experiments
- **Identify drug candidates** — that might otherwise go unnoticed
- **Reduce timelines** — from years to months

## Key techniques

### Machine Learning for Target Identification

ML models can sift through thousands of scientific papers and datasets to identify promising drug targets:

| Technique | Application | Impact |
|-----------|-------------|--------|
| Deep learning | Protein structure prediction | 10x faster |
| NLP | Literature mining | Broad coverage |
| Graph neural nets | Molecular property prediction | High accuracy |

### Molecular Docking with AI

Traditional docking is computationally expensive. AI models can approximate binding affinities in milliseconds:

$$
E_{binding} \approx f_{NN}(molecule, protein)
$$

## Getting started

If you are an African researcher interested in incorporating AI into your drug discovery workflow, here are practical first steps:

1. **Learn the basics** of Python and machine learning
2. **Explore open-source tools** like RDKit, DeepChem, and our own Magami toolkit
3. **Reach out to us** for collaboration and training opportunities

## The road ahead

AI will not replace the creativity and domain expertise of African researchers. But it will amplify it. Our mission is to ensure that every researcher on the continent has access to these capabilities.
